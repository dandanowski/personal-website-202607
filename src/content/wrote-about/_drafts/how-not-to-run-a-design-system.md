I've been managing the product design system at Atolls for just about three years now. On the final stretch before converting to the next iteration of the design system which has many breaking changes, I'd like to take stock of all that I learned while maintaining the production version, including what didn't go well (many things) and what did (there were a few).

<!-- excerpt -->

---

## Run it like a product, 'cause it is
Any good product needs to have a goal, objective, and a good understanding of the users it serves. This will help communicate the direction of the design system, it'll also help others anticipate what might be in or out of scope.

Provide a roadmap of some sort to help manage expectations and let dependant teams know when they can rely on the different elements and output your design system will have. This could take the form as a detailed project in a ticketing system such as Jira,  Microsoft Azure DevOps, Asana, or Monday.com.
